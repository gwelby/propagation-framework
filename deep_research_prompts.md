## PROMPT 1: Does the Koide Formula Generalize?

### The Question

The Koide formula relates the three charged lepton masses with extraordinary precision (0.0009% from 2/3). We confirmed this numerically. Now: does ANY version of this formula hold for other particle families? Specifically:

- Do the three up-type quark masses (up, charm, top) satisfy a Koide-like relation?
- Do the three down-type quark masses (down, strange, bottom) satisfy one?
- Do the three neutrino masses (if we use current best-fit values) satisfy one?
- Has anyone published generalizations of Koide to other families?
- What is the theoretical status of the Koide formula as of 2024-2026? Is it still considered a coincidence or has anyone derived it from first principles?

### Why It Matters

If the Koide relation generalizes across particle families, it's strong evidence that the particle mass spectrum is constrained by something structural — consistent with the propagation framework's claim that particles are resonance modes of a medium whose geometry constrains their frequencies relative to each other.

If it's unique to charged leptons, it might be a coincidence — or it might indicate something special about the electromagnetic coupling structure.

### What Would Confirm the Framework

Koide-like relations (within 1%) for other particle families. Or a derivation from first principles that involves resonance structure, standing wave conditions, or boundary constraints in a field theory.

### What Would Falsify

Definitive proof that the Koide formula is a numerical coincidence with no physical content. Or demonstration that quark masses are too scheme-dependent (MSbar running masses) for any fixed algebraic relation to be meaningful.

### Starting References

- Koide, Y. (1981) — original paper
- Rivero & Gsponer (2005) — extensions to quarks
- Any 2024-2026 papers citing Koide — check arXiv hep-ph
- Particle Data Group 2024 — current mass values

## PROMPT 4: Coherence-Consciousness Co-occurrence — How Tight Is It Really?

### The Question

The Propagation Framework claims consciousness requires coherence and that coherence disruption always co-occurs with consciousness disruption. This is presented as the framework's main testable prediction. How well does it actually hold?

Do a rigorous literature search:

- Anesthesia and cortical coherence: what exactly is measured? How tight is the correlation? Are there exceptions?
- Sleep stages and coherence: does coherence track consciousness level through NREM stages and REM?
- Disorders of consciousness (vegetative state, minimally conscious state): what do coherence measures show?
- Split-brain patients: what happened to coherence and to consciousness?
- Psychedelics: these reportedly INCREASE some forms of consciousness while DISRUPTING coherence — is this a counterexample? What exactly happens to coherence under psilocybin/LSD/DMT?
- Meditation: experienced meditators show increased gamma coherence — does this correlate with reported changes in conscious experience?
- Are there ANY documented cases of high coherence without consciousness, or consciousness without coherence?
- What coherence measure matters? Phase-locking value? Spectral coherence? Granger causality? Different measures might give different answers.

### Why It Matters

This is the one prediction the consciousness portion of the framework makes that can actually fail. If a single clear counterexample exists — high coherence with no consciousness, or rich consciousness with disrupted coherence — the framework's consciousness claim is in trouble.

The psychedelic case is the most dangerous for the framework. Psychedelics are reported to expand consciousness while disrupting some coherence patterns. If this holds up under scrutiny, the framework needs to either refine its definition of coherence or acknowledge a failure.

### What Would Confirm

Tight co-occurrence across all measured conditions. No counterexamples. Psychedelic effects explained by redistribution of coherence (disrupting some patterns while creating new ones) rather than loss of coherence.

### What Would Falsify

Documented case of full cortical coherence in a system with no consciousness (e.g., a seizing brain has high synchrony — is that coherence? Is there consciousness during a generalized seizure?). Or documented rich conscious experience during measured coherence disruption.

### Starting References

- Tononi, G. — Integrated Information Theory, phi as a coherence measure
- Casali et al. (2013) — Perturbational Complexity Index for consciousness
- Carhart-Harris et al. — "The entropic brain" — psychedelics and neural dynamics
- Mashour & Hudetz — "Neural Correlates of Unconsciousness in Large-Scale Brain Networks"
- Any 2024-2026 reviews on consciousness and neural synchrony/coherence

### What Would Falsify

## PROMPT 6: Matter as Standing Waves — What Does QFT Actually Say?

### The Question

The Propagation Framework claims particles are standing waves — self-reinforcing propagation patterns. It claims this is "what QFT already says." But is it? Really?

Get rigorous about this:

- In quantum field theory, is a particle literally a standing wave, or is it an excitation of a field that has some wave-like properties?
- What is the precise relationship between a particle and a resonance in QFT?
- The Compton wavelength/frequency — what is its physical meaning? Is it literally the oscillation frequency of the particle, or is it a scattering parameter?
- Zitterbewegung (trembling motion) — Schrödinger predicted that a free electron oscillates at its Compton frequency. Is this observed? Is it real?
- The "particle as standing wave" picture — does it hold for fermions (half-integer spin), or only for bosons? Fermions obey the Pauli exclusion principle, which standing waves don't obviously do.
- What specifically does the Higgs mechanism do to standing wave modes? Does it change their frequency (mass) without changing their structure?
- Confinement in QCD — is this literally describable as "extreme refraction" as the framework claims, or does the framework break here?

### Why It Matters

"Matter is standing waves" is one of the framework's strongest and most fundamental claims. If QFT genuinely supports it (not just loosely but technically), the framework is reading existing physics correctly. If QFT says something importantly different, the framework is over-claiming.

### What Would Confirm

Technical QFT literature that explicitly describes particles as resonances or standing wave modes of quantum fields. Zitterbewegung observed experimentally. Particle properties (spin, charge, mass) derivable from standing wave boundary conditions.

### What Would Falsify

QFT experts explaining why the standing wave picture fails for fermions, or for interacting theories, or for gauge bosons. Demonstration that "excitation of a field" is importantly different from "standing wave in a medium."

### Starting References

- Peskin & Schroeder — "An Introduction to Quantum Field Theory" (the standard textbook)
- Hestenes, D. — "The Zitterbewegung Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics"
- Wilczek, F. — "The Lightness of Being" (accessible but rigorous on QFT ontology)
- Any 2024-2026 foundational papers on QFT interpretation / particle ontology

- Analog cosmology — has anyone simulated expanding universe dynamics in a lab medium?

- Has anyone used analog gravity to make a NEW prediction about actual gravity that was then tested?

### Why It Matters

Analog gravity is the most direct experimental test of the framework's core claim — that gravity IS refraction, not merely analogous to it. If lab experiments faithfully reproduce gravitational phenomena in propagation media with controlled gradients, the framework's ontological claim is strengthened. If the analogy breaks at a specific point, we need to know where.

### What Would Confirm

Precision measurements of analog Hawking radiation matching theoretical predictions. New gravitational predictions generated from analog experiments and subsequently confirmed in astrophysics. Expanding catalog of gravitational phenomena reproducible in analog media.

### What Would Falsify

A specific gravitational phenomenon that cannot be reproduced in any analog medium even in principle — demonstrating that gravity has properties beyond what any propagation medium can produce.

### Starting References

- Unruh (1981) — original sonic analog proposal
- Steinhauer (2016, 2019) — BEC acoustic black hole experiments
- Barceló, Liberati, Visser — "Analogue Gravity" Living Reviews

## PROMPT 8: Phase Transitions in Neural Systems — Is Insight a Critical Phenomenon?

### The Question

The Propagation Framework predicts that insight — the sudden reorganization of cognitive structure — should have the statistical signature of a critical phase transition. Not just "a burst of gamma." Actual critical exponents, power-law distributions, diverging correlation lengths.

Has anyone tested this?

- Beggs & Plenz (2003) demonstrated neuronal avalanches with power-law distributions. Has this been connected to insight or Aha moments?
- The gamma burst at insight (Jung-Beeman et al. 2004) — has anyone analyzed it for critical exponent signatures rather than just frequency content?
- Criticality in neural networks — what's the current status? Is the "critical brain hypothesis" supported, weakened, or refined by 2024-2026 work?
- Long-range temporal correlations in EEG — do these change around moments of insight?
- Is there a neural equivalent of "diverging correlation length" at the moment of insight — i.e., does the spatial extent of correlated neural activity suddenly increase?
- Representational change theory of insight (Ohlsson) — can representational change be formalized as a phase transition in a state space?

### Why It Matters

If insight is a critical phase transition, the Propagation Framework's prediction about "threshold phenomena at the causal velocity" is confirmed for neural systems. If insight is just "increased gamma" without critical signatures, the framework's phase transition claim is too strong.

### What Would Confirm

Critical exponent analysis of EEG data around insight moments showing power-law statistics. Diverging correlation length at insight onset. Computational models showing insight as a phase transition in neural network state space.

### What Would Falsify

Insight EEG data showing simple frequency increase without power-law signatures. Or demonstration that "critical brain" is not actually near criticality but in a different dynamical regime.

### Starting References

- Beggs & Plenz (2003) — neuronal avalanches
- Shew & Plenz (2013) — functional benefits of criticality
- Kounios & Beeman (2014) — "The Aha! Moment" review
- Fontenele et al. (2019) — criticality in cortical dynamics
- Any 2024-2026 papers connecting neural criticality to cognition

## PROMPT 11: Contemplative Practices as Empirical Propagation Framework Discoveries

### The Question

Five thousand years of contemplative traditions — Vedic, Taoist, Sufi, Shamanic, Hermetic, Gnostic, Sanskrit — independently developed practices (mantra, breathwork, drumming, whirling, movement, meditation) that produce measurable changes in how humans process information and experience reality. Modern neuroscience can now measure what these practices actually do.

The specific question: are these practices, described in modern neuroscience terms, **frequency modulation and coherence enhancement protocols for the neural propagation medium**?

Specifically:
- What does peer-reviewed neuroscience (2015-2026) show about the measurable effects of meditation, breathwork, chanting, and rhythmic movement on neural oscillation frequencies, coherence, and information processing?
- Does slow breathing (4-6 breaths/min) demonstrably entrain higher-frequency neural oscillations toward coherence via baroreceptor feedback? (Bottom-up frequency locking claim)
- Does shamanic drumming (4-4.5 Hz) reliably shift subjects toward theta-band processing? What changes in information processing accompany this shift?
- What happens to the boundary between self-perception and environment during high-coherence states? Does the "dissolution" experience correspond to measurable changes in default mode network / boundary processing?
- Does the effective information sampling rate (in the Shannon sense) change with meditation practice or altered states? Has anyone measured information density in EEG signals before/after coherence-inducing practices?
- Can the Shannon channel capacity framework (bandwidth = information capacity) be directly applied to neural oscillation bands? Has anyone attempted this explicitly?

### Why It Matters

Greg's observation: a depressed person brings the room down, a high-energy person lifts it. If people are propagation systems and emotional states are coherence states, then person-to-person interaction involves **wavefront coupling at biological boundaries** — reflection, refraction, absorption, emission between two coherent systems. The contemplative traditions figured this out empirically. The framework predicts it structurally.

If this research confirms:
1. That contemplative practices are measurably frequency modulation / coherence protocols
2. That Shannon's channel capacity applies to neural bands (more coherence = more information per cycle)
3. That HRV / physiological state propagates across people in shared spaces

...then the framework has just explained why 5,000 years of independent human traditions converged on the same set of techniques. Not mysticism. Empirical discovery of propagation physics in the only medium the practitioners had access to.

### What Would Confirm

- Peer-reviewed studies showing breathwork directly alters neural oscillation coherence (not just frequency)
- EEG studies showing information density changes (permutation entropy, Lempel-Ziv complexity) correlate with coherence state changes from contemplative practice
- HRV synchronization between people in shared spaces documented beyond individual coupling
- Any paper explicitly mapping a contemplative practice to an information-theoretic measure
- Neuroscience of chanting / mantra showing carrier frequency entrainment

### What Would Falsify

- Coherence changes from contemplative practice are purely subjective / don't appear in objective EEG measures
- No relationship between neural oscillation coherence and measured information processing quality
- HRV synchronization between people is documented only during direct interaction, not ambient shared space
- Shannon framework cannot be meaningfully applied to neural bands (category error)

### The Specific Prediction to Test

Greg's "upshift/downshift" observation: humans modulate carrier frequency within the range their substrate supports (40 Hz gamma upper limit, ~0.05 Hz breath lower limit). The ancient traditions are empirical maps of this modulation space.

**Testable prediction:** Contemplative practices that ancient traditions describe as "elevating" should correspond to measurable increases in gamma coherence. Practices described as "grounding" or "going inward" should correspond to measurable decreases toward theta. The subjective vocabulary should map onto the frequency spectrum systematically, across independent traditions.

If that mapping is consistent across Vedic, Sufi, Shamanic, and Taoist traditions — traditions that had no contact with each other — that is extraordinary evidence that they were all measuring the same underlying phenomenon.

**Control condition (critical):** Track historical contact between traditions as a confound variable. Vedic and Buddhist practices mapping similarly is expected — they share extensive lineage. Vedic and Amazonian shamanic practices mapping similarly is extraordinary — zero historical contact. Independent convergence is the real signal. Shared lineage is cultural transmission. The research agent must explicitly distinguish which tradition pairs had contact and which didn't, and weight the cross-tradition convergence evidence accordingly. Convergence without contact is what makes this scientific rather than diffusion.

### Starting References

- Lutz, A. et al. — "Long-term meditators self-induce high-amplitude gamma synchrony during mental practice" (2004, PNAS) — baseline for gamma coherence in meditation
- McCraty, R. et al. — HeartMath Institute studies on HRV coherence and social field effects
- Hove, M.J. & Risen, J.L. — synchrony and social bonding literature
- Shannahoff-Khalsa, D. — "Kundalini Yoga Meditation" — one of the few practitioners who published neuroscience-standard measurements
- Shannon, C.E. — "A Mathematical Theory of Communication" (1948) — the original channel capacity theorem Greg re-derived from lived experience
- Any 2020-2026 review of "neural oscillation coherence" + "information processing"
- Winkelman, M. — "Shamanism: A Biopsychosocial Paradigm of Consciousness and Healing" — systematic mapping of shamanic states to neuroscience

### Note on Framing

Do NOT approach this as "does mysticism work." Approach it as: **"Given that these practices demonstrably produce measurable physiological and cognitive effects, what is the best current mechanistic explanation, and does that explanation map onto propagation framework concepts?"**

The practices are data. The mystical interpretations are the pre-scientific theory. The question is whether the propagation framework is a better theory for the same data.
