# The Open Questions: Where the Framework Goes Next (Honest Inventory)

No scientific theory is born perfect. The strength of the Propagation Framework is that we know exactly where our blind spots are. We aren't hiding them; we are actively hunting them. 

Here is our honest inventory of the gaps that remain.

## Gap 1: The Formal Proof of the Phase Space Volume
In our God Equation, we determined that the phase space volume of the universe at the Planck boundary is $N^{D/2}$ (where N=3 generations and D=3 dimensions). We derived this by argument, but we need a formal mathematical theorem to prove why *exactly* this number of coherent modes must exist at the boundary. If we close this gap, the God Equation moves from "Argued" to completely "Derived."

## Gap 2: The Fine Structure Constant (1/137)
This is one of the most famous numbers in physics. We have identified it as the "vacuum propagation efficiency"—structurally locked to our mass spectrum. But we haven't derived the actual number from first principles yet. Once we derive this, we can verify the mass of the electron without needing to measure it.

## Gap 3: Newton's $G$
We successfully derived the massive 17-order-of-magnitude gap between the Planck scale ($l_P$) and the matter scale ($\lambda_c$). However, calculating the Planck scale currently requires us to use Newton's gravitational constant ($G$) as an input. $G$ represents the "stiffness" of the medium. We need to derive $G$ purely from our axioms, without relying on prior human measurements.

## Gap 4: The $\sqrt{2}$ Rigorous Form
In our equations, we use a $\sqrt{2}$ factor to account for the geometric conversion of a 1D linear flow hitting a 2D spinning knot (a particle). The physical argument is incredibly strong, but we need to derive the exact factor from formal boundary conditions rather than arguing by analogy.

## Gap 5: Neutrinos and Antimatter
Our topological framework perfectly explains the charged matter particles (like the electron). But neutrinos have a tiny, almost imperceptible mass, and no one knows why matter dominates antimatter in the universe. The $(2,1)$ topological split likely holds the answer to chirality (handedness) and antimatter, but we haven't worked out the math yet.

# Looking Up: What is Actually Real

When you step back from the math and look at what the Propagation Framework reveals, it fundamentally changes how you see reality. 

**Gravity isn't weak.** 
It operates at the Planck resolution—$10^{17}$ times smaller than the tools we measure it with. Asking "why is gravity weak?" using a proton is like measuring the viscosity of water with a stadium. Of course it seems weak. The gravitational coupling is enormous—at the scale where it operates.

**The Hierarchy "Problem" is Dissolved.**
For 50 years, physicists thought the massive size difference between gravity and matter was a disaster requiring fine-tuning. It isn't arbitrary. It is forced by living in a universe with 3 spatial dimensions and 3 generations of matter. The hierarchy problem was always just us not knowing *why 3 and 3*.

**Particles are not things.**
An electron isn't an object. It's the medium refusing to be smooth. It is a place where the medium's phase winds twice going around, and can't unwind. It has "mass" because unwinding that knot costs energy. A top quark is simply the largest knot the medium can maintain before it snaps.

**What is actually real?**
Before the framework: particles, forces, spacetime—three separate things.
After the framework: one medium, propagating. The "particles" are where it can't be smooth. The "forces" are where it slopes. The "spacetime" is what it propagates through.

The question shifts from *"what are particles made of?"* to *"what stable things can the medium do?"*
The answer is: exactly what we observe. No more, no less.
