## Findings

### 1. The Thermal Time Hypothesis (Connes & Rovelli)

**Origin**: Alain Connes (mathematician) and Carlo Rovelli (physicist) proposed that time emerges from thermodynamics.

**Core Claim**: 
- Time is defined in terms of thermal equilibrium states
- In canonical quantum gravity, the world appears "fundamentally timeless"
- Time emerges when a coarse-grained state determines thermal dynamics

**Current Status (2024-2025)**:
- Active area of philosophical and technical investigation
- Recent paper (arXiv:2407.18948, July 2024) titled "The Time in Thermal Time" examines the hypothesis
- **Major criticism**: Circularity worry — thermal equilibrium presupposes dynamics, which presupposes time. Therefore time is required to define time.
- Published in *Journal for General Philosophy of Science* (forthcoming, special issue on "On Time in the Foundations of Physics")

**Key Insight for Propagation Framework**:
The thermal time hypothesis shares the propagation framework's core intuition: time is not fundamental but derived. However, it derives time from thermodynamics/entropy, while the propagation framework derives it from propagation geometry.

**Experimental Tests**: None directly. The hypothesis is primarily conceptual/philosophical.

### 2. Page-Wootters Mechanism (Time from Entanglement)

**Origin**: Don Page and William Wootters (1983) — "Evolution without evolution"

**Core Claim**:
- The universe is globally timeless
- Time emerges from entanglement between subsystems
- One subsystem acts as a "clock" that measures evolution of another
- Correlations between clock and system create the appearance of temporal order

**Current Status (2024-2026)**:
- **Extended framework** (Favalli et al., arXiv:2512.08120, December 2025): Now describes both time AND space emergence from entanglement
- Reproduces gravitational effects (Schwarzschild time dilation) from pure quantum entanglement
- Suggests spacetime itself may be emergent rather than fundamental

**Experimental Demonstrations**:
- Photon entanglement experiments where one photon acts as a clock
- Successfully modeled time dilation effects consistent with General Relativity
- Tested both "observer mode" (internal, correlated with clock) and "super-observer mode" (external, viewing global timeless system)

**Key Insight for Propagation Framework**:
This is the most technically developed "time is emergent" program with experimental support. The propagation framework's claim that "time is what propagation feels like from inside" is structurally similar to Page-Wootters' "time is what entanglement correlations look like from inside a subsystem."

**Limitations**:
- Relies on specific assumptions about initial conditions
- Relies on specific assumptions about the nature of entanglement
- Further investigation needed for full implications

### 3. Julian Barbour's Timeless Physics

**Origin**: Julian Barbour, *The End of Time* (1999), *The Janus Point* (later)

**Core Claim**:
- Time does not exist at all
- Only "Nows" exist — complete instantaneous configurations of the universe
- The feeling of temporal flow is imposed by us as we organize Nows into sequences
- "Time capsules" are Nows that contain rich internal structure (memories, fossils, records) that appear to reference other Nows

**Technical Foundations**:
- **Machian Dynamics**: Motion/inertia defined by relations between objects, not absolute space or clocks
- **Shape Dynamics**: Alternative gravity formulation emphasizing evolving shape of space over spacetime
- **Janus Point**: A moment where universal complexity reaches minimum; structure increases in both directions away from it, creating two arrows of time

**Current Reception**:
- **Appreciated**: The "problem of time" in quantum gravity is real; relational/Machian approach has genuine technical content; shape dynamics is a serious research direction
- **Skepticism**: Most physicists accept timelessness as a *formalism feature*, not an *ontological verdict*; explanatory gaps remain (how do records recover full causal/relativistic structure?); limited testability

**Key Insight for Propagation Framework**:
Barbour's "Nows" are essentially frozen snapshots. The propagation framework disagrees: the fundamental thing is not the snapshot but the propagation itself. Time is not an illusion — it's real from inside a propagation medium, just not fundamental from outside.

### 4. Carlo Rovelli's Relational Quantum Mechanics and Loop Quantum Gravity

**Core Claims** (from Quanta Magazine interview, October 2025):
- Time is not fundamental — it emerges from more basic physical processes
- Thermodynamic origin: our experience of time flowing forward is a product of entropy increase (second law)
- **Perspectival reality**: The flow of time is like the rotation of the sky — real from our perspective, not fundamental from outside
- In loop quantum gravity equations: **no time variables, no space variables**
- Space = quantity of loops; Time = how loops evolve continuously

**Connection to Buddhist Philosophy**:
Rovelli influenced by Nagarjuna (Buddhist philosopher) — no "ultimate absolute reality"; things only exist depending on one another (relational ontology)

**Experimental Implications**:
- **Main shortcoming**: Lack of experiments supporting LQG
- **Proposed tests**: 
  - Cosmic Microwave Background signatures
  - Microscopic black holes (~10 micrograms) — might be dark matter
  - Earlier versions predicted wavelength-dependent light speed (contradicted by data); current version compatible with constant c

**Key Insight for Propagation Framework**:
Rovelli's "relational" view aligns with propagation framework's claim that time is observer-relative (depends on sampling path through propagation field). However, Rovelli derives time from entropy/thermodynamics, while propagation framework derives it from propagation geometry.

### 5. Emergent Time in Quantum Gravity (General)

**Loop Quantum Gravity**:
- Discretizes spacetime through spin network states
- Faces the "problem of time" inherent in canonical quantization
- Recent work (arXiv:2502.18524v5, January 2026): "Emergent spacetime from spatial energy potentiality"
- LQG formalism robustly resolves classical singularities in symmetry-reduced models

**Other Approaches**:
- **Unified Time Theory** (preprints.org, May 2025): Time as emergent property of entropy, drawing on LQG and holographic principles
- **Harlow-Usatyuk-Zhao paper** (MIT/Kavli, November 2025): "The universe could be simple; we might be what makes it complicated" — complexity from observer perspective, not fundamental laws

**Key Insight for Propagation Framework**:
The "problem of time" is widely recognized in quantum gravity. Multiple independent programs derive time as emergent. The propagation framework is not alone in this intuition — but it may be unique in deriving time specifically from propagation geometry rather than entropy, entanglement, or configuration space structure.

### 6. Critical Brain Hypothesis and Temporal Perception

**Critical Brain Hypothesis**:
- Brain operates near a critical point (phase transition) between order and chaos
- At criticality: optimal computational capacity, pattern complexity, sensitivity to perturbation
- **Neuronal avalanches**: cascades of neural activity with power-law size distributions (Beggs & Plenz, 2003)

**Recent Findings (2024-2025)**:
- **Maschke et al.** (Nature Communications Biology, August 2024): Critical dynamics in EEG predict anesthetic-induced unconsciousness
  - Propofol/xenon (abolish consciousness): shift brain to **subcritical regime** (branching ratio < 1)
  - Ketamine (preserves consciousness despite unresponsiveness): **maintains near-critical dynamics**
  - Resting-state criticality metrics predicted Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI) with **mean error < 7%**
  - Conclusion: "Criticality is a necessary condition for the emergence of consciousness"

- **CNS*2025** (34th Annual Computational Neuroscience Meeting): Critical avalanches arise through balanced amplification at a phase transition

- **Temporal Integration Windows**:
  - Auditory cortex integration windows: ~15-150 ms (Cell, December 2025)
  - "Specious present" (William James' term for the psychological present moment) mediated by reentrant oscillatory processes
  - Multisensory integration during speech perception occurs over windows of hundreds of milliseconds

**Key Insight for Propagation Framework**:
The propagation framework predicts that consciousness requires coherent self-referential propagation. The critical brain findings strongly support this: consciousness co-occurs with criticality (a specific coherence state), and consciousness disruption always co-occurs with criticality disruption. This is the tightest empirical support for the propagation framework's consciousness claim.

**Connection to Time**:
The "specious present" — the psychological present moment — may be the temporal scale at which coherent propagation patterns integrate. If time is what propagation feels like from inside, then the integration window (~100ms) is the timescale of a single "propagation cycle" in cortical tissue.

## Summary: The Landscape

**Who else sees time as emergent?**
- **Yes, multiple programs**: Thermal time (Connes & Rovelli), Page-Wootters (entanglement), Barbour (timeless physics), LQG (no time variables in fundamental equations)
- **Not alone**: The propagation framework's intuition that "time is not fundamental" is shared by serious research programs in quantum gravity and foundations of physics

**What makes propagation framework different?**
- **Specific derivation**: Time from propagation geometry, not from entropy (thermal time), entanglement (Page-Wootters), or configuration space structure (Barbour)
- **Observer-relative but real**: Time is not an illusion (Barbour) — it's real from inside a propagation medium, just not fundamental from outside
- **Geometric arrow**: Time's arrow is propagation geometry (expanding wavefronts), not (just) entropy increase

**What empirical support exists?**
- **Page-Wootters**: Photon entanglement experiments, reproduces time dilation
- **Critical brain**: Tight correlation between criticality and consciousness (Maschke et al., 2024)
- **Thermal time**: None directly — conceptual/philosophical
- **Propagation framework**: None directly yet — but critical brain findings support the coherence-consciousness claim

**What's the reception?**
- **Timeless/emergent time programs**: Taken seriously in quantum gravity circles, but not mainstream
- **Mainstream view**: Time may not be fundamental (sympathy), but strong ontological claims (Barbour's "no time at all") face skepticism
- **Testability**: The main challenge for all emergent time programs

**Novelty assessment**:
- The claim "time is emergent" is **not novel** — multiple programs make this claim
- The specific claim "time IS propagation (from inside a medium)" appears **novel** — no direct matches in literature
- The propagation framework's geometric derivation of time's arrow (expanding wavefronts) appears **novel** — distinct from entropy-based derivations
