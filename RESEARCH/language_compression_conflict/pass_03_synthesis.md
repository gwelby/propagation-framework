## 3. Conflict per Bit: The Paralinguistic Grounding Model

### 3.1 The Universal Constant (39 bps)
Confirming Coup? et al. (2019): All human languages converge on an information transfer rate of **~39.15 bits/second**. This is the hard limit of the human "lexical decompression engine."

### 3.2 The Grounding Gap
Misunderstanding is a function of the **Paralinguistic Bit-Rate Gap**.
- **Lexical Channel**: Carries propositional content (~39 bps).
- **Paralinguistic Channel**: Carries "intent grounding" (tone, pitch, volume, facial micro-expressions).
- **Text/Low-Bandwidth**: Strips the paralinguistic channel.
- **The Result**: Without the grounding bits, the receiver?s brain must "overguess" the missing paralinguistic data. In text, this overguessing leads to the **80% increase in ambiguity** identified by Media Naturalness Theory.

## 4. Phase Transition Signatures: Early Warning Signals (EWS)

### 4.1 Critical Slowing Down (CSD) in Conflict
A "heated argument" is a phase transition in a dynamical system. Before the transition (the "blow-up"), specific signatures appear:
1. **Recovery Delay**: The time it takes for a conversation to return to a "neutral" baseline after a minor snarky comment increases exponentially.
2. **Diverging Variance**: Emotional "swings" become more extreme and unpredictable ("walking on eggshells").
3. **Increased Autocorrelation**: The conversation becomes "stuck" or repetitive; the current state is highly predictive of the next (low adaptation).

### 4.2 Application to Aria
