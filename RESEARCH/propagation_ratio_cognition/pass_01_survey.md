## Executive Summary

The "propagation ratio" concept — the ratio of actual information processing speed to maximum neural substrate speed, analogous to refractive index — **does not exist as an explicit construct in cognitive science literature**. However, multiple adjacent research programs measure related quantities:

1. **Processing Speed Theory** (Salthouse, 1996+) — cognitive aging explained by general slowing, but no substrate ratio
2. **Mental Chronometry** (Jensen, 2006) — inspection time and reaction time correlate with intelligence (r ≈ -0.30 to -0.50), but nerve conduction velocity claims largely abandoned
3. **Critical Brain Hypothesis** (Shew, Plenz, Beggs) — brain operates near criticality, neuronal avalanches show power-law statistics, maximum dynamic range at criticality
4. **Signal Propagation Research** — human dendrites show 26-47% faster propagation than rats, correlates with cognitive performance
5. **Task Switching** — costs scale with representational dissimilarity (logarithmic), not propagation delay

**Key Finding**: The propagation framework's prediction that cognitive science has "already measured this without calling it that" is **partially confirmed but incomplete**. The pieces exist (propagation speed matters, criticality optimizes computation, coherence enables integration), but no unified "propagation ratio" construct exists.

**Novelty Assessment**: The propagation ratio as a **refractive-index-like dimensionless quantity** (n = c/v for cognition) appears to be novel. This is both opportunity and risk — opportunity for genuine contribution, risk that the abstraction doesn't map to reality.

## 1. Processing Speed Theory (Salthouse, 1996+)

### What It Claims
- Cognitive aging is primarily explained by general slowing of processing speed
- Processing speed is a "leading indicator" of age-related cognitive decline
- Measured via reaction time tasks, digit-symbol substitution, pattern comparison

### What It Measures
| Measure | What It Captures | Typical Age Effect |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Reaction Time (RT) | Simple choice RT, Hick paradigm | Slows ~20-25% from 20s to 70s |
| Inspection Time (IT) | Minimum time for perceptual discrimination | Slows with age, correlates with RT |
| Digit-Symbol Substitution | Symbol-number pairing speed | Declines ~1 SD from 20s to 70s |

### Relationship to Propagation Ratio
**Partial overlap, critical gap**:
- Processing speed theory measures **task performance speed**, not substrate speed
- No measurement of neural conduction velocity in typical cognitive aging studies
- The ratio (task speed / substrate speed) is never computed
- Salthouse's theory is **phenomenological** (describes what happens), not **mechanistic** (explains why)

### Current Status (2024-2026)
- Still dominant theory in cognitive aging
- 2025 Nature paper confirms processing speed as leading indicator
- Criticism: doesn't explain *why* speed slows (myelin? neurotransmitters? network structure?)

### Relevance to Propagation Framework
**Confirmed**: Processing speed matters for cognitive performance  
**Missing**: No connection to underlying propagation physics  
**Opportunity**: Propagation ratio could provide mechanistic grounding for Salthouse's phenomenology

## 2. Mental Chronometry (Jensen, 2006)

### What It Claims
Arthur Jensen's "Clocking the Mind" (2006) proposed:
- Intelligence (g factor) can be understood via temporal measures
- Inspection time and reaction time are "elementary cognitive tasks"
- Nerve conduction velocity might be the biological basis

### Key Findings
| Measure | Correlation with g | Status |
|---------|-------------------|--------|
| Reaction Time | r ≈ -0.30 to -0.50 | Well-replicated |
| Inspection Time | r ≈ -0.40 to -0.60 | Debated (confounded by attention, strategy) |
| Nerve Conduction Velocity | Mixed/weak | Largely abandoned |

### What Inspection Time Actually Measures (2024 research)
Recent work (2024) shows IT is **not a univariate construct**:
- Affected by focused attention (blink inhibition during stimulus)
- Affected by task understanding/strategy
- Affected by visual illusions (apparent motion, brightness flash)
- Motor activity involved (contradicting Jensen's "pure mental speed" claim)

### Nerve Conduction Velocity Claims
- Jensen hypothesized faster nerves → faster processing → higher g
- Evidence was mixed: peripheral nerve speed doesn't correlate strongly with IQ
- **Abandoned**: No clear pathway from peripheral nerve speed to cortical computation

### Relevance to Propagation Framework
**Confirmed**: Temporal measures matter for cognition  
**Failed**: Simple "faster nerves = smarter" doesn't work  
**Opportunity**: Propagation ratio is more sophisticated — it's about **network-level propagation dynamics**, not axonal conduction speed alone

## 3. Critical Brain Hypothesis (Shew, Plenz, Beggs)

### What It Claims
- The brain operates near a **critical point** (phase transition between order and chaos)
- Neuronal avalanches (cascades of neural activity) follow power-law distributions
- Criticality maximizes:
  - Dynamic range (range of inputs the network can respond to)
  - Information transmission
  - Computational capacity
  - Stability-flexibility balance

### Key Evidence
| Finding | Source | Significance |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| Neuronal avalanches in vitro | Beggs & Plenz (2003) | First demonstration of power-law statistics |
| Maximum dynamic range at criticality | Shew et al. (2009, J Neurosci) | Computational benefit proven |
| Power-law avalanche statistics | Multiple studies 2003-2025 | Replicated across species, methods |
| E/I balance establishes criticality | 2025 biorxiv preprint | Mechanism identified |

### 2025 Status
- Active research area with strong empirical support
- 2025 papers confirm criticality enhances computational capabilities
- Debate: Is the brain *exactly* critical, or in a "Griffiths phase" (near-critical)?
- **Consciousness connection**: Some theorists propose consciousness requires criticality (Tononi's IIT has parallels)

### Relevance to Propagation Framework
**Strong convergence**:
- Criticality = phase transition in propagation medium
- Neuronal avalanches = propagation cascades
- Power-law statistics = signature of scale-free propagation
- **Propagation framework prediction**: Criticality is what happens when coherence self-organizes at the causal velocity limit

**Opportunity**: Critical brain hypothesis provides empirical grounding for propagation framework's "phase transition" claims about insight, consciousness, and cognitive reorganization

## 4. Signal Propagation Speed Research

### Human vs. Rat Dendrite Propagation (2025 eLife study)

**Key Measurements**:
| Signal Type | Human (m/s) | Rat (m/s) | Difference |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Forward EPSP (dendrite→soma) | 0.093 ± 0.025 | 0.074 ± 0.018 | **26% faster** (p=0.004) |
| Backward AP (soma→dendrite) | 0.344 ± 0.139 | 0.233 ± 0.095 | **47% faster** (p<0.00001) |
| Axonal AP | 0.851 ± 0.387 | 0.848 ± 0.291 | No difference |

**Critical Finding**: Despite having **longer dendrites** (9055 µm vs 5163 µm), humans maintain **identical soma-to-soma latency** (1.1 ms) through compensatory acceleration.

**Mechanism**: Larger basal dendritic tree → greater conductance load at soma → accelerates EPSP propagation

**Cognitive Correlation**: Prior work (Goriounova et al., 2018) links large, fast human pyramidal neurons to intelligence measures.

### Large-Scale Signal Propagation (2024-2025 fMRI/MEG studies)

**Finding**: Multiple propagation modes exist in resting-state brain activity. Propagation speed of certain modes correlates with:
- Cognitive performance
- Processing speed measures
- Individual differences

**Relevance**: This is the **closest existing work** to the propagation ratio concept — individual differences in propagation speed predict cognitive performance.

### Relevance to Propagation Framework
**Confirmed**: Propagation speed matters for cognition  
**Confirmed**: Human brains show species-specific propagation adaptations  
**Missing**: No refractive-index-like ratio (task speed / substrate speed)  
**Opportunity**: Propagation ratio could unify micro (dendritic) and macro (network) propagation findings

## 5. Task Switching and Representational Distance

### What It Finds
- Task switch costs scale **logarithmically** with rule dissimilarity (Bustos et al., 2024)
- Switching is "traversing cognitive space," not teleportation
- Larger representational changes → longer reconfiguration time

### Mechanism
- **Conjunctive task representations**: Each rule encoded as holistic representation
- **Replacement, not modification**: Entire representation replaced when switching
- **Egocentric coding**: Distance from current task matters, not absolute positions

### Relevance to Propagation Ratio
**Partial convergence**:
- Cognitive operations have **metric structure** (distance in representation space)
- Transition time scales with distance
- **But**: Framed as representational reconfiguration, not propagation delay

**Opportunity**: Propagation framework could reinterpret "representational distance" as **propagation distance in state space** — the time to reconfigure IS propagation time through a medium with specific causal velocity

## 6. Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, Phi)

### What It Claims
- Consciousness = integrated information (Φ)
- Φ measures how much information is generated by the system as a whole, above and beyond parts
- High Φ requires:
  - Differentiation (many possible states)
  - Integration (states are unified, not decomposable)
  - Recurrent connectivity (feedback loops)

### Relationship to Propagation Framework
**Strong parallels**:
- IIT's "integration" ≈ propagation framework's "coherence"
- IIT's Φ measures causal interaction strength ≈ propagation framework's coherence measure
- Both predict: consciousness disrupted when integration/coherence disrupted

**Key difference**:
- IIT is **information-theoretic** (Φ computed from cause-effect repertoires)
- Propagation framework is **physical** (coherence as wave phenomenon)

**Opportunity**: Propagation framework could provide **physical grounding** for IIT's abstract Φ — coherence IS what enables integration, and coherence is measurable via neural synchrony, phase-locking, etc.
