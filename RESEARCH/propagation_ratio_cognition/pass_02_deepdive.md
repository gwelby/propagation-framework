## 1. The Propagation Ratio: Found in the "Refraction Ratio"

The framework's prediction of a refractive-index-like ratio is confirmed by the **Refraction Ratio** ($R$) used in neuro-engineering (notably UC San Diego research).

### The Metric
- **Formula**: $R = \frac{\text{Refractory Period}}{\text{Signal Latency}}$
- **Refractory Period**: The time a neuron needs to "reset" after firing (the medium's recovery time).
- **Signal Latency**: The time for the signal to travel the axon (the propagation time).
- **The "Perfect" Index**: Biological systems optimize for **$R \approx 1.0$**.
  - **$R < 1$ (Sub-optimal)**: Signal arrives before the receiver is ready (bottleneck/interference).
  - **$R > 1$ (Inefficient)**: Receiver is idle, wasting potential throughput.

### Predictive Power
- This ratio predicts **Signaling Efficiency**. It explains why axon length and diameter are precisely tuned (myelination) to match the recovery cycles of the receiving neurons. 
- **Cognitive Mapping**: Deviations from $R=1$ likely correlate with "Cognitive Noise" and "Processing Lag" observed in aging and pathology.
