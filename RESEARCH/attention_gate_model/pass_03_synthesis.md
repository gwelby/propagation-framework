## 2. Refractive Mapping — The Attention Lenses of the DAN

The Dorsal Attention Network (DAN) acts as a dynamic "refractive lens" by modulating the **phase gradient** of cortical traveling waves.

### 2.1 Traveling Waves as Information Carriers
-   **Top-Down (DAN $\rightarrow$ Sensory)**: Alpha/Beta waves (8–20 Hz) propagate frontal-to-posterior. They carry predictive priors and "gate" incoming signals.
-   **Bottom-Up (Sensory $\rightarrow$ DAN)**: Theta/Gamma waves (4–100 Hz) propagate posterior-to-frontal. They carry stimulus data.

### 2.2 The Attention Lens Mechanism
-   **Phase Gradient Modulation**: Attention does not just increase gain; it **shifts the phase gradient** ($k$) of these traveling waves.
-   **Routing**: By changing $k$, the DAN controls the "arrival time" of the wave at specific cortical columns. This creates "attention lenses" that ensure distant nodes are phase-aligned at the exact moment of computation (impedance matching).
-   **Phase Velocity ($v_p$)**: Typically 0.1–2.0 m/s in human cortex.
