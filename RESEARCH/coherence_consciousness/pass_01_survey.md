## Executive Summary

This survey mapped the major players, frameworks, and empirical findings in consciousness-coherence research. The landscape is dominated by two major theoretical frameworks (Integrated Information Theory and Global Neuronal Workspace), substantial empirical work on anesthesia and disorders of consciousness, emerging research on psychedelics, and well-established findings on meditation and neural synchrony.

**Key finding**: The coherence-consciousness link is **well-supported empirically** but with critical nuance: consciousness requires *differentiated integration* (balance of coherence and specialization), not just high synchrony. Pathological over-synchronization (seizures) destroys consciousness.

---

## Major Theoretical Frameworks

### 1. Integrated Information Theory (IIT) — Tononi, Boly, Casali

**Core claim**: Consciousness = integrated information (Φ). A system is conscious to the extent it generates information above and beyond its parts.

**Key concepts**:
- **Φ (Phi)**: Mathematical measure of integrated information
- **PCI (Perturbational Complexity Index)**: TMS-EEG measure of brain response complexity; robust empirical marker across species
- **Axioms → Postulates**: IIT derives physical requirements from phenomenal properties (experience itself as proof of existence)
- **IIT 4.0**: Latest version (2025) emphasizes "consciousness-first" ontology; claims cause-effect structure accounts for ALL properties of experience (spatial extendedness, temporal flow, object binding, qualia)

**Status**: Most mathematically rigorous framework. Strong empirical support from PCI measurements.

**Connection to Propagation Framework**: IIT's integration requirement maps to propagation framework's coherence axiom. However, IIT is more specific about the *structure* of integration (differentiated, not uniform).

---

### 2. Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW) — Dehaene, Changeux

**Core claim**: Consciousness results from coherent neuronal activity between widely distributed brain regions. Information becomes conscious when broadcast globally via "workspace" neurons.

**Key concepts**:
- **Communication Through Coherence (CTC)**: Fries' mechanism — neural regions communicate effectively when oscillations are phase-coherent
- **Broadcasting network**: GNW implements CTC for global information sharing
- **Frontal-parietal networks**: Critical for conscious access

**Status**: Dominant framework in cognitive neuroscience. Strong empirical support from attention/awareness studies.

**Connection to Propagation Framework**: GNW's "global broadcast" is literally coherent propagation across cortical networks. Direct mapping to propagation framework's coherence axiom.

---

### 3. Critical Brain Hypothesis — Beggs, Plenz, Shew

**Core claim**: Brain operates near critical phase transitions, enabling optimal information processing.

**Key concepts**:
- **Neuronal avalanches**: Power-law distributed neural activity bursts (Beggs & Plenz 2003)
- **Critical exponents**: Signatures of phase transitions in neural data
- **ConCrit Framework** (2025): Links consciousness specifically to critical dynamics

**Status**: Active research area. Strong computational support. Empirical evidence mixed but growing.

**Connection to Propagation Framework**: Criticality = propagation threshold phenomenon. Direct mapping to propagation framework's prediction about "threshold phenomena at causal velocity."

## Empirical Findings by Domain

### Anesthesia & Loss of Consciousness

**What happens to coherence**:

| Measure | Effect During Anesthesia | Notes |
|---------|-------------------------|-------|
| **Gamma oscillations** (30-80+ Hz) | Suppressed (propofol, volatiles); Induced (ketamine) | Disruption associated with sensory processing loss |
| **Alpha coherence** (8-12 Hz) | Hypercoherent in prefrontal cortex | Entrained oscillation isolates prefrontal from corticocortical communication |
| **Long-range connectivity** | Disrupted | Frontal-parietal disconnection consistent across anesthetics |
| **Thalamus ↔ Association cortex** | Disrupted | Especially prefrontal connections |
| **Thalamus ↔ Primary sensory** | Maintained | "Information received but not perceived" |
| **Feedback connectivity** | Selectively suppressed | Feedforward relatively spared |
| **PCI (Perturbational Complexity)** | Decreases | Robust marker of consciousness level |

**Key mechanism**: Anesthetized brain shifts away from critical dynamics — patterns become less complex, more tethered to structural connectivity, network efficiency decreases.

**Source**: Multiple 2024-2026 reviews (PMCID: PMC11098701, Engineering 2021, BJA Open 2023)

---

### Disorders of Consciousness (Vegetative State, Minimally Conscious)

**Findings**:
- **Spectral signatures**: MCS patients retain partial conscious awareness with measurable coherence differences from PVS
- **PCI**: Robustly distinguishes vegetative state from minimally conscious state
- **EEG coherence spectra**: Raw coherence measures show differences but less robust than PCI
- **Nonlinear analysis**: Interconnection measures of residual cortical functional islands show promise

**Status**: Active clinical research. PCI is emerging as gold standard for consciousness assessment.

**Source**: Neurology Today 2025, PMC4217390, multiple 2023-2026 studies

---

### Epilepsy & Seizures — Critical Counterexample

**Finding**: **Excessive synchrony destroys consciousness.**

During generalized seizures:
- **Mean Shortest Path** ↓ (excessive integration)
- **Modularity** ↓ (loss of specialization)
- **ΦAR** ↓ (integrated information decreases)
- **Consciousness** lost

**Correlation**: r = 0.78 (p < 5e-05) between ΦAR drop and consciousness loss in non-compromised regions.

**Critical insight**: Consciousness requires **differentiated integration** — balance of coherence AND specialization. Seizures represent pathological over-coherence.

**Source**: PMC10912751 (2024)

---

### Sleep Stages

**Findings**:
- **NREM sleep**: Coherence increases during slow-wave sleep (stages 2-4)
- **REM sleep**: Coherence patterns resemble wakefulness more than NREM
- **EEG functional connectivity**: Reflects sleep stages and changes in consciousness
- **Developmental trajectory**: Sleep EEG coherence increases through childhood/adolescence

**Status**: Well-established literature. Coherence tracks consciousness level through sleep stages, but relationship is complex (high coherence in deep NREM doesn't imply high consciousness).

**Source**: PMC12413340 (2025), Springer 2025, Nature 2023

---

### Meditation & Long-Term Practitioners

**Landmark finding**: Lutz et al. (2004) — Long-term Buddhist practitioners self-induce sustained high-amplitude gamma-band oscillations and phase synchrony during meditation.

**Replicated findings**:
- **30% increase** in long-distance phase synchrony in practitioners
- **Gamma coherence**: Highest reported in non-pathological conditions
- **Open monitoring meditation**: Alters frontoparietal gamma coherence in experts vs. novices

**Status**: Well-replicated. Meditation increases coherence, particularly gamma-band.

**Connection to Propagation Framework**: Direct support for coherence-consciousness link. Meditators voluntarily induce coherence states associated with altered/enhanced conscious experience.

**Source**: PubMed 15534199, Academia.edu, ScienceDirect 2022

---

### Psychedelics — Dangerous Test Case

**Entropic Brain Hypothesis** (Carhart-Harris): Psychedelics increase brain entropy, associated with expanded consciousness.

**Recent findings** (2024-2026):
- **Psilocybin**: Triggers activity-dependent rewiring of large-scale cortical networks
- **Connectome changes**: Not monolithic — composite of multiple network effects
- **13 studies** evaluated psychedelic effects on brain entropy (medRxiv 2026)

**Critical question for Propagation Framework**: Do psychedelics increase or decrease coherence?

**Preliminary answer**: Mixed effects — some coherence patterns disrupted, others enhanced. "Redistribution" rather than simple loss. This is the most dangerous empirical territory for the framework.

**Source**: BioRxiv 2024, Cell 2025, medRxiv 2026, Neuropharmacology 2018

---

### Split-Brain Patients

**Landmark finding** (UCSB 2025): **Minimal corpus callosum connections can unify consciousness.**

- One patient with only ~1cm intact posterior fibers showed **zero disconnection effects**
- Brain reorganizes over ~1 year to find "unusual routing"
- **Posterior section** appears critical for integration
- Full synchrony possible with surprisingly few fibers

**Implications**:
- Coherence is **threshold-based, not linear**
- Critical minimum exists — once crossed, full coherence emerges
- Propagation finds available paths (refraction-like behavior)

**Source**: UCSB News 2025, The Scientist 2025

### Interpersonal Synchronization — Emerging Area

**HRV Coherence** (HeartMath):
- HRV coherence biofeedback improves stress resilience, autonomic function
- 20+ independent studies in 2025
- **Social synchronization**: Heart rates synchronize during close-proximity interactions (bioRxiv 2025)

**Interpersonal neural synchrony**:
- fNIRS hyperscanning shows inter-brain synchrony during interaction
- Developmental framework emerging (2025)

**Status**: Early but promising. Direct relevance to propagation framework's prediction about person-to-person wavefront coupling.

**Source**: HeartMath 2026, Nature Scientific Data 2025, bioRxiv 2025

## Challenges & Counterexamples

### For Propagation Framework's Coherence-Consciousness Claim

| Challenge | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| **Seizures**: High synchrony, LOW consciousness | **Critical** — requires framework refinement (differentiated integration, not just coherence) |
| **Deep NREM sleep**: High coherence, low consciousness | **Moderate** — may be different TYPE of coherence |
| **Psychedelics**: Disrupted coherence, EXPANDED consciousness | **Critical** — needs careful analysis of which coherence patterns change |
| **Anesthesia**: Ketamine induces gamma but causes LOC | **Moderate** — suggests gamma alone insufficient |

### Framework Absorbs These With Refinement

The propagation framework claim should be refined:
- **Not**: "More coherence = more consciousness"
- **But**: "Consciousness IS coherent self-referential propagation with differentiated integration"

This matches IIT's core insight (integration + specialization) and explains seizures (too much integration, lost specialization).
