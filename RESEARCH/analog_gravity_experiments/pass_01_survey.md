## Executive Summary

Analog gravity is a mature, active research field with **multiple experimental platforms**, **dozens of active research groups worldwide**, and **demonstrated laboratory reproduction of gravitational phenomena**. The field has moved from theoretical proposal (Unruh 1981) to experimental demonstration (Steinhauer 2016-2019, multiple groups since) to current focus on **quantum entanglement verification** and **cosmological applications**.

**Key finding for Propagation Framework**: Analog gravity directly tests the claim that "forces are refraction" — if curved spacetime effects can be reproduced in laboratory media with controlled propagation gradients, this strengthens the ontological claim that gravity IS refraction, not merely analogous to it.

### Platform Comparison

| Platform | What It Simulates | Achievements | Limitations |

| **Water Wave Tanks** | Black/white holes, horizons | ✅ Classical Hawking radiation observed (Weinfurtner)<br>✅ Low-cost, accessible<br>✅ Visual demonstration | ❌ Classical only (no quantum effects)<br>❌ Limited to surface waves<br>❌ Dissipation effects |

## 6. Limitations and Criticisms

### Known Limitations of Analog Gravity

| Limitation | Description | Source |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| **Not actual Hawking radiation** | Analog confirms mathematical robustness, not astrophysical reality | Arstechnica 2019 |
| **Trans-Planckian problem unresolved** | Simulations show radiation survives atomic-scale dispersion, but black hole quantum gravity regularization is unknown | Nappo & Cangiotti |
| **Cannot access quantum gravity regime** | Analog tests classical/semi-classical approximations only | Nappo & Cangiotti |
| **No known common causal mechanism** | Kinematic alignment (horizons, waves) but dynamical generation differs | Nappo & Cangiotti |
| **Cannot establish universality class** | No way to prove black holes and dumb holes are in same universality class | Nappo & Cangiotti |
| **Back-reaction untested** | How created particles affect spacetime dynamics — open problem | Glasgow QTG |
| **No new gravitational predictions** | No case where analog experiment predicted astrophysical phenomenon later confirmed | Survey finding |

### Philosophical Status

From Nappo & Cangiotti (arXiv:2212.13501v2):
- Analog simulations provide **"limited but genuine incremental confirmation"** via material analogy
- They **"integrate, but cannot replace, more direct forms of evidence"**
- Evidence is **conditional** on no critical differences between source (black hole) and target (analog)

## 7. Connection to Propagation Framework

### What Analog Gravity Tests

| Propagation Framework Claim | Analog Gravity Connection | Status |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------|--------|
| **Forces are refraction** | Analog gravity = refraction in controlled media reproducing gravitational effects | ✅ Directly tested |
| **Curved spacetime = propagation gradient** | Analog systems use refractive index gradients to simulate curvature | ✅ Demonstrated |

| **Time = propagation metric** | Not directly tested by analog gravity | ⚪ Open |

### Where Analog Gravity Supports the Framework

1. **Refraction-as-force**: Analog gravity demonstrates that **refraction in a medium with controlled gradients reproduces gravitational phenomena** — directly supporting the claim that "forces are refraction."

2. **Causal velocity centrality**: Every analog system has an explicit causal velocity (speed of sound in BEC, speed of light in fiber) — supporting Axiom 2.

3. **Robustness across platforms**: Hawking radiation appears in BEC, water, optics, metamaterials — suggesting it's a **universal propagation phenomenon**, not specific to gravity.

### Where Analog Gravity Does NOT Test the Framework

1. **Ontological claim**: Analog gravity shows mathematical equivalence, but doesn't prove gravity IS refraction (could be merely analogous).

2. **Back-reaction**: Framework predicts propagation-medium coupling, but analog gravity hasn't tested this yet.

3. **New predictions**: Framework should predict gravitational phenomena reproducible in analog media that haven't been observed — no such prediction yet made.
