## Executive Summary

The fine structure constant (α ≈ 1/137) and its relationship to particle masses is an **active, unresolved area of physics** with multiple competing approaches. The literature falls into three broad categories:

1. **Empirical mass formulas** — Koide-type relations extended to include α (Greulich, Tangherlini, Kosinov)
2. **Impedance/propagation derivations** — α as ratio of vacuum impedances (Bosa, various independent authors)
3. **Deflationary accounts** — α as contingent structural coupling, not fundamental (Sticker 2025)

**Key finding**: The Propagation Framework's core claim — that constants like α emerge from propagation properties of the vacuum medium — has **multiple independent research programs pursuing similar ideas**. None have achieved consensus, but the convergence of approaches is notable.

## 1. The Koide Formula & Alpha Connection

### 1.1 Kosinov (2024) — Direct Koide-Alpha Link

**Source**: ESS Open Archive (Oct 2024)  
**Status**: Preprint, peer-reviewed status unknown

**Thesis**: The Koide formula for lepton masses is directly connected to the fine structure constant α.

**Specific claim**: Beyond the standard Koide relation:
$$Q = \frac{m_e + m_\mu + m_\tau}{(\sqrt{m_e} + \sqrt{m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\tau})^2} = \frac{2}{3}$$

There exists an extended formula incorporating α that relates particle masses to the electromagnetic coupling strength.

**Access issue**: Full text unavailable (403 Forbidden). Multiple secondary sources confirm existence but not details.

**Relevance to Propagation Framework**: If Koide and α are derivable from the same underlying structure, this supports the framework's claim that both mass spectrum and coupling constants emerge from vacuum propagation properties.

### 1.2 Tangherlini (2022) — Alpha from Mass Ratios

**Source**: Journal of Modern Physics, Vol.13 No.5, May 2022  
**Status**: Peer-reviewed journal publication

**Core formula**:
$$\alpha^2 + \frac{\alpha}{f} - \frac{13.5}{f}\frac{m_e}{m_p} = 0$$

Where $f$ is a dimensionless parameter constructed from particle mass ratios.

**Best accuracy claimed**: Δα/α ≈ 5.5×10⁻⁹ (formula #7 in table)

**Methodology**:
- Assumes electron has "bare mass" with finite electromagnetic self-energy
- Searches for particle mass combinations that yield correct α
- All formulas are homogeneous degree zero in masses (scale-invariant)

**Key insight**: The parameter $f$ is always of form:
$$f = 1 + \frac{m_{numerator}}{\sum_i m_i}$$

Using various combinations of hadron and lepton masses.

**Relevance**: Demonstrates that α can be expressed as a function of particle mass ratios — exactly the type of relationship the Propagation Framework would predict if both masses and coupling constants emerge from the same vacuum structure.

### 1.3 Greulich — The Alpha/Beta Rule

**Source**: Multiple conference proceedings (2014-2025), most recent ResearchGate (Feb 2026)  
**Status**: Conference contributions, not mainstream peer-reviewed

**Claim**: "The masses of essentially all elementary particles are given almost exactly by the α/β rule"

**Formula structure**:
- Particle masses depend on α (≈ 1/137)
- Requires empirical beta term: β = 1 or 1836.12

**Key insight**: 
- Bohr energy = (1/α) × Rydberg energy (13.6 eV)
- Electron rest energy / Bohr energy = α⁻²
- Extrapolated to all particles

**Accuracy**: Claimed "almost exactly" — no specific precision given in available abstracts.

**Relevance**: Most ambitious mass formula program — claims all particles, not just leptons. The empirical β term is a weakness (not derived).

## 2. Impedance/Propagation Approaches

### 2.1 Bosa (2026) — Spacetime Impedance Framework

**Source**: Preprints.org (Jan 2026)  
**Status**: Preprint

**Framework**: Treats spacetime as distributed RLC medium with complex impedance:
$$Z(ω) = R_H + jωL_H - \frac{1}{ωC_H}$$

**Alpha derivation**:
$$\alpha = \frac{1}{4π} \cdot \frac{Z_0}{R_Q}$$

Where:
- $Z_0 = \sqrt{μ_0/ε_0} ≈ 376.73 Ω$ (classical vacuum impedance)
- $R_Q = ℏ/e² ≈ 25.813 kΩ$ (quantum resistance)

**Physical interpretation**: α quantifies the **impedance mismatch** between classical and quantum vacuum regimes.

**Key result**: 
$$\frac{R_K}{Z_0} = \frac{1}{2α} ≈ 68.5$$

Where $R_K = h/e²$ is the von Klitzing constant (quantum Hall resistance).

**Relevance to Propagation Framework**: **Extremely high**. This is literally the framework's claim — α emerges from propagation properties (impedance) of the vacuum medium. The RLC model maps directly to propagation concepts:
- Inductance $L_H$ → resistance to temporal flux change
- Capacitance $C_H$ → spatial coupling, polarization
- Resistance $R_H$ → irreversibility, entropy

### 2.3 Non-Linear Vacuum Resonance Model

**Source**: Academia.edu (2024)  
**Status**: Self-published

**Model**: Vacuum as non-linear medium supporting spherical oscillators with resonance shells.

**Derivation**:
- Base geometry: circumference = 2π × radius (but 2π isn't integer)
- Orbit stabilizes at radius where circumference = 861 × Coulomb radius
- Relativistic correction yields enlargement factor
- Result: 1/α* = 137.0360020051429...

**Claimed accuracy**: ~12 significant figures vs. quantum Hall measurements

**The number 861**: Sum of integers 1 to 41, where 41 is from Euler's prime-generating polynomial (n² + n + 41).

**Relevance**: "Particles as vacuum resonances" matches Propagation Framework's "matter is standing waves" claim. The 861 derivation is structurally similar to Koide's algebraic approach.

**Caution**: Number-theoretic derivations risk numerology — needs independent verification.

## 3. Deflationary Accounts

### 3.1 Sticker (2025) — Alpha as Scaled Quantity

**Source**: arXiv:2512.07027 (Dec 2025)  
**Status**: arXiv preprint

**Thesis**: α is **not a fundamental dimensionless constant** but a **scaled quantity** arising from intersection of three theoretical frameworks:
- Classical electromagnetism (e)
- Quantum mechanics (ℏ)
- Special relativity (c)

**Key argument**:
- α first appears when Sommerfeld added relativistic corrections to Bohr hydrogen
- α becomes coupling parameter in QED
- The "running" of α in QED (energy-dependent variation) proves it's scale-dependent, not universal

**Conclusion**: α is "contingent structural coupling," not derivable from first principles.

**Relevance**: Supports Propagation Framework's deflationary stance on constants, but stops short of providing a derivation. The claim that α is "contingent" is compatible with the framework's view that α emerges from specific vacuum propagation properties.

## 4. Running Coupling & Vacuum Polarization

### 4.1 QED Running of α

**Established physics**: The fine structure constant "runs" with energy scale due to vacuum polarization.

**Value at different scales**:
- Low energy (atomic): α ≈ 1/137.036
- MZ scale (~91 GeV): α ≈ 1/127

**Mechanism**: Virtual electron-positron pairs in vacuum polarize the medium, screening the charge.

**Relevance to Propagation Framework**: The running of α is **direct evidence that the vacuum has frequency-dependent propagation characteristics**. This is exactly what the framework predicts for a medium: propagation properties depend on frequency.

**Key insight**: If α runs with energy, it cannot be truly fundamental — it's a property of the vacuum medium's response to propagation at different scales.
