## Executive Summary

This survey investigated the Propagation Framework's claim that **"matter is stable self-reinforcing propagation patterns — standing waves in the fabric"** and that this is "what QFT already says." 

**Key Finding**: The landscape is **nuanced**. Mainstream QFT describes particles as **excitations of quantum fields** with wave-like properties, but there is active debate about whether "standing wave" is literally accurate or merely analogical. The field-centric ontology is dominant, but the precise relationship between fields and particles remains philosophically contested.

**Confidence**: 0.65 — Substantial alignment exists, but important technical distinctions remain.

## 2. What Exists: The Current Landscape

### 2.1 Standard QFT Ontology (Textbook View)

**Consensus Position**:
- **Fields are fundamental**; particles are excitations of fields
- Particles are **quantized** (discrete energy packets) but **not point-like** in the classical sense
- Energy-frequency relation: **E = hf is an identity**, not a proportionality
- Particles are **indivisible units** that travel as coherent wavepackets

**From Quanta Magazine (2022)**:
> "Particles are localized, resonant excitations of these fields, vibrating like springs in an infinite mattress."

**From Physics Says What (2024)**:
> "Elementary particles are vibrating entities... An electron is never a dot — always spread out to some degree, always vibrating."

### 2.2 The "Wavicle" Position (Strassler 2024)

**Critical Distinction**: Matt Strassler argues that calling particles "standing waves" is **technically inaccurate** for several reasons:

| Property | Classical Standing Wave | QFT Wavicle |
|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| Energy adjustable via amplitude? | Yes | **No** — energy fixed by frequency |
| Can exist without container? | No (needs boundaries) | **Yes** — massive particles can be stationary in empty space |
| Frequency determined by? | Container size | **Mass** (f = mc²/h for stationary electron) |

**Strassler's Bottom Line**:
> "A wavicle does have features of both waves and particles, but it also lacks features of both. I would prefer to say that it is **neither one**."

### 2.3 The Zitterbewegung Interpretation (Hestenes)

**Claim**: Schrödinger predicted (1927) that free electrons undergo rapid oscillatory motion ("trembling motion") at the **Compton frequency** (~10²¹ Hz).

**Hestenes's Position**:
- Zitterbewegung is **real physical oscillation**, not mathematical artifact
- Electron is literally a **circulating light-like charge** at Compton scale
- Spin and magnetic moment arise from this internal motion
- **Direct standing wave interpretation**: Electron is self-contained oscillatory structure

**Experimental Status**: 
- Observed in **trapped ion simulations** (Gerritsma et al., 2010)
- **Not directly observed** in free electrons (frequency too high for current detectors)
- Controversial whether this is "real" or effective description

### 2.4 Wilczek's "Ether" Rebranding

**Historical Claim**: Wilczek argues "ether" got a bad reputation undeservedly. QFT fields are **direct descendants** of the luminiferous ether concept:

- Both fill all space
- Both have vibrational modes
- Both determine properties of "empty" space

**From NYT (1999)**:
> "Wilczek maintains that ether has undeservedly been given a bad name, when in fact, it should be regarded as direct ancestor of quantum fields."

**Modern Framing**: Wilczek calls it "**the Grid**" — a multilayered superconducting medium from which quanta emerge as topological excitations.

## 4. Key Trends

### 4.1 Shift Toward Field-Centrism

**Trend**: Moving away from "particle physics" language toward "quantum field theory" as the fundamental description.

**Evidence**:
- Strassler's 2024 book explicitly argues for field-first ontology
- Wilczek's decades-long advocacy for "ether re-emergence"
- PBS NOVA criticized (2024) for using "quantum mechanics" 16 times but never mentioning fields

**Implication for Propagation Framework**: Strong alignment — the field-centric view is gaining ground in mainstream physics communication.

### 4.2 "Wavicle" as Third Category

**Trend**: Rejecting wave-particle duality language as obsolete; adopting "wavicle" or similar third-category terms.

**Evidence**:
- Strassler: "Neither wave nor particle"
- Vedral: Coupled pendulum analogy (neither purely wave nor purely particle)
- Standard textbooks increasingly avoid duality language

**Implication**: Propagation Framework's "standing wave" language may be **too strong** — "coherent excitation" or "self-reinforcing propagation pattern" is more precise.

### 4.3 Resurgence of Deterministic Underpinnings

**Trend**: Some researchers (t'Hooft, Penrose) exploring whether QM is statistical approximation to deeper deterministic theory.

**Evidence**:
- Gerard 't Hooft (Nobel laureate): "Superpositions are not real... just a trick that works"
- Roger Penrose: Objective collapse at Planck scale
- Hestenes: Zitterbewegung as real deterministic motion

**Implication**: Propagation Framework's deterministic framing (propagation as fundamental, not probabilistic) has **some precedent** but remains minority view.

### 4.4 Relational/Contextual Properties

**Trend**: Properties (mass, spin, charge) increasingly viewed as **relational** rather than intrinsic.

**Evidence**:
- Rovelli's relational QM: properties only meaningful relative to other systems
- Mass as frequency (E=hf) — frequency is relational (depends on observer frame)
- Koide formula derivations (Varma 2026, Bin Li 2025) — mass ratios from topological relations, not intrinsic values

**Implication**: Strong alignment with Propagation Framework's coherence-based ontology.

## 6. What Would Confirm the Propagation Framework

### Strong Confirmations (Would Significantly Strengthen)

1. **Direct observation of Zitterbewegung** in free electrons at Compton frequency
2. **Derivation of particle masses** from standing wave boundary conditions (Koide-style but generalized)
3. **Experimental demonstration** that particle properties change with "container" geometry (beyond known Casimir effects)
4. **Formal proof** that QFT geodesic equations are mathematically identical to Fermat's principle in all cases

### Weak Confirmations (Already Present)

1. ✅ Field-centric ontology dominant
2. ✅ E=hf treated as identity
3. ✅ Particles described as "vibrations," "resonances," "excitations"
4. ✅ Wilczek's "ether" rebranding gains some traction

### What Would Falsify (Would Weaken or Require Revision)

1. **Proof that standing wave picture fails for fermions** (Pauli exclusion incompatible with standing waves)
2. **Demonstration that "excitation" is categorically different from "standing wave"** (not just semantically)
3. **Direct observation of point-like structure** at scales below Compton wavelength
4. **Experimental evidence for intrinsic properties** independent of relational context

## 7. Open Gaps (What Needs More Research)

### High Priority

1. **Fermion vs. Boson Standing Wave Compatibility**
   - Does the standing wave picture hold for half-integer spin particles?
   - Pauli exclusion principle — do standing waves naturally exclude, or is additional structure required?
   - **Gap**: No clear literature deriving Pauli exclusion from pure standing wave boundary conditions

2. **Zitterbewegung Experimental Status**
   - What exactly was observed in trapped ion experiments?
   - Is there any direct evidence in free electrons?
   - **Gap**: No comprehensive review of Zitterbewegung experimental claims post-2020

3. **QCD Confinement as "Extreme Refraction"**
   - Propagation Framework claims confinement is refractive gradient increasing with distance
   - Does this match actual QCD mathematics (asymptotic freedom, flux tubes)?
   - **Gap**: No technical mapping between propagation framework refraction equations and QCD Lagrangian

4. **Higgs Mechanism in Standing Wave Terms**
   - Propagation Framework says Higgs changes "frequency (mass) without changing structure"
   - Does this match actual Higgs mechanism (Yukawa coupling, spontaneous symmetry breaking)?
   - **Gap**: No derivation showing Higgs mass generation = frequency shift in standing wave picture

### Medium Priority

5. **Gauge Bosons as Standing Waves**
   - Photons cannot form standing waves in infinite space (per Strassler)
   - Does this break the "all particles are standing waves" claim?
   - **Gap**: Clarification needed on what "standing wave" means for massless particles

6. **Particle Creation/Annihilation**
   - How do standing waves "pop in and out" of existence (pair production, annihilation)?
   - Is there a standing wave interpretation of creation/annihilation operators?
   - **Gap**: No clear standing wave ontology for second quantization

7. **Interacting vs. Free Field Theory**
   - Most standing wave analogies use free field theory (non-interacting)
   - Does the picture hold for interacting theories (real world)?
   - **Gap**: No standing wave interpretation of Feynman diagrams, propagators, scattering amplitudes
