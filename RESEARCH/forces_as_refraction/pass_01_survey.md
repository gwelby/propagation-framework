## Executive Summary

The "forces as refraction" claim has **substantial mathematical support** in established physics literature, but the **ontological claim** (identity vs. analogy) remains unresolved. Three major research programs are directly relevant:

1. **Analog Gravity** (Unruh 1981 → Barceló/Liberati/Visser 2005-2025) — demonstrates that propagation in media can reproduce gravitational kinematics (horizons, Hawking radiation, geodesics) with mathematical precision
2. **Transformation Optics** (Leonhardt, Piwnicki, Pendry 1999-2026) — shows that curved spacetime light propagation is mathematically equivalent to light in metamaterials with spatially varying refractive index
3. **Variational Principles** (Fermat, Maupertuis, Hamilton, Lagrange) — establishes that the geodesic equation in GR and the ray equation in optics both derive from stationary-action principles

**Key finding**: The mathematical equivalence is well-established. The ontological question (is gravity *literally* refraction in some medium, or merely *analogous* to refraction?) is not settled by existing literature. The propagation framework's claim goes beyond what analog gravity proves.

## 1. Analog Gravity — What Has Been Demonstrated

### 1.1 Core Discovery (Unruh 1981)

**Paper**: "Experimental Black Hole Evaporation" (Phys. Rev. Lett. 46, 1351, 1981)

**Finding**: Sound waves in a moving fluid obey a wave equation identical to a massless scalar field in curved spacetime. When fluid flow becomes supersonic, sound cannot escape upstream → creates an **acoustic event horizon**.

**The acoustic metric**:
```
g_μν = (ρ/c) × [-(c²-v²), -vᵗ; -v, I]
```

This is not an approximation. It is an exact mathematical identity for the kinematic behavior of sound in a moving fluid.

### 1.2 The Living Reviews Article (Barceló, Liberati, Visser)

**Status**: The definitive review of analog gravity. Originally published 2005, updated 2011. Cited extensively in 2020-2026 literature.

**What has been demonstrated**:

| Phenomenon | System | Status |
|------------|--------|--------|
| Acoustic horizons | Moving fluids, BECs | ✅ Experimentally realized |
| Hawking radiation (phononic) | BECs (Steinhauer 2014-2024) | ✅ Observed (spontaneous emission) |
| Ergo-regions | Rotating fluids | ✅ Theoretically complete |
| Cosmological particle production | Expanding BECs | ✅ Demonstrated |
| Super-radiance | Rotating systems | ✅ Theoretically modeled |
| Quasinormal modes | Various | ✅ Measured |

**What has NOT been demonstrated**:

| Limitation | Consequence |
|------------|-------------|
| No dynamical gravity | Metric determined algebraically from fluid variables, not by Einstein equations |
| Degrees of freedom mismatch | GR has 6 DOF per point; acoustic metrics have 2-3 |
| Fixed background topology | Cannot model topology change, closed timelike curves |
| No back-reaction | No analogue of "matter tells spacetime how to curve" |

### 1.3 Jeff Steinhauer's BEC Experiments (2014-2024)

**Key papers**:
- Nature Physics 10, 864 (2014) — acoustic black hole laser
- Recent work (2024-2025) — thermal Hawking radiation observation

**Finding**: Spontaneous Hawking radiation observed in Bose-Einstein condensates with acoustic horizons. The radiation is thermal with temperature proportional to surface gravity.

**Significance**: Hawking radiation is a **kinematic phenomenon** — it does not depend on Einstein's equations, only on the existence of a horizon and quantum fields. This is the strongest result from analog gravity.

### 1.4 The Critical Quote (on analogy vs. identity)

From the Living Reviews article:

> "Of course analogy is not identity, and we are in no way claiming that the analogue models we consider are completely equivalent to general relativity — merely that the analogue model (in order to be interesting) should capture and accurately reflect a sufficient number of important features of general relativity."

**Interpretation**: Analog gravity demonstrates that **media can mimic gravity** with mathematical precision. It does not demonstrate that **gravity is a medium**. The direction of explanation matters.

## 2. Transformation Optics — Curved Spacetime as Metamaterial

### 2.1 Leonhardt & Piwnicki (1999-2002)

**Key papers**:
- "Optics of nonuniformly moving media" (1999)
- "Geometrical approach to light in inhomogeneous media" (2002)

**Finding**: A moving dielectric appears to light as an **effective gravitational field**. The correspondence is exact in the geometric optics limit.

**Key result**: Light in a medium with refractive index n(x) follows the same paths as light in a curved spacetime with metric:
```
ds² = (1/n²)dt² - dx² - dy² - dz²
```

This is not an approximation. It is an identity for null geodesics.

### 2.2 Recent Work (2020-2026)

**Paper**: "Gravitational Metamaterials from Optical Properties of Spacetime Media" (Luongo, arXiv:2504.09987, April 2025)

**Claims**:
- Spherically-symmetric spacetimes behave as media with definable refractive indices
- Two different refractive index definitions are related by coordinate transformation
- Snell's law applies in gravitational contexts
- Speculation: gravitational metamaterials may have **negative refractive index** (light bent opposite to normal direction)
- Conjecture: particle-like configurations ("geometric quasiparticles") contribute to dark matter

**Language used**: The paper treats spacetime as **literally having optical properties**, not as analogous to them. Phrases like "spacetimes behaving as media" and "equivalence with nonlinear effects induced by geometry" suggest identity, not analogy.

### 2.3 Roadmap on Electromagnetic Metamaterials (2025)

**Paper**: "Roadmap on electromagnetic metamaterials and metasurfaces" (IOP Science, 2025)

**Relevance**: Confirms that transformation optics and general relativity are mathematically equivalent frameworks. The roadmap explicitly notes the similarity between TO and GR, with metamaterials able to study relativity phenomena such as gravitational lensing.

## 3. Variational Principles — Fermat, Maupertuis, Hamilton, Lagrange

### 3.1 The Optical-Mechanical Analogy

**Key sources**:
- Evans, J. "The optical-mechanical analogy in general relativity" (1996-2002)
- Multiple papers confirming the equivalence

**Finding**: Fermat's principle (light takes the path of stationary optical length) and Maupertuis' principle (particles take the path of stationary action) are **mathematically identical** when applied to static gravitational fields.

**The geodesic equation in GR** can be derived from Fermat's principle applied to a medium with spatially varying propagation speed. This is not new. Einstein used this.

### 3.2 What This Means

| Principle | Domain | Mathematical Form |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Fermat's principle | Optics | δ∫n(x)dl = 0 |
| Maupertuis' principle | Mechanics | δ∫p dq = 0 |
| Hamilton's principle | General | δ∫L dt = 0 |
| Geodesic equation | GR | δ∫ds = 0 |

All four are variational principles. All four yield Euler-Lagrange equations. The mathematical structure is identical.

**For the propagation framework**: This supports the claim that forces (gravity included) are refraction-like phenomena derivable from stationary-propagation principles.

## 4. Standard Model Forces — Variational Formulation

### 4.1 Gauge Fields and Variational Principles

**Finding**: The Standard Model is founded on local gauge symmetry and derived from variational principles. The equations of motion for gauge fields (electromagnetism, weak, strong) all derive from δS = 0.

**Relevance to propagation framework**: If all four forces derive from variational principles, and if those principles are equivalent to Fermat-like stationary-propagation conditions, then the "forces as refraction" claim has a pathway to unification.

**Gap identified**: No literature found that explicitly derives all four forces from a single Fermat-like principle applied to a multi-component propagation medium. This is where the propagation framework could make a novel contribution.

## 5. Related Research Programs

### 5.1 Process Ontology (Whitehead, Wheeler, Verlinde)

**Whitehead's process philosophy** (1929): Reality is made of events ("actual occasions"), not substances. Directly parallels the propagation framework's claim that propagation is fundamental.

**Wheeler's "it from bit"** (1990): Information is fundamental; spacetime and particles emerge from informational processes.

**Verlinde's emergent gravity** (2011): Gravity is not fundamental — it emerges from entropy and information considerations (entropic gravity).

**Relevance**: These are independent programs that share the propagation framework's core intuition: what we treat as fundamental (particles, forces, spacetime) may be emergent from deeper structures.

### 5.2 Critical Brain Hypothesis (Beggs & Plenz, 2003-2026)

**Finding**: Neuronal networks show power-law distributed "avalanches" characteristic of systems near phase transitions. Recent work (2025) links criticality to consciousness (ConCrit framework).

**Relevance to propagation framework**: If consciousness requires coherence, and if critical systems maximize coherence/correlation length, then the critical brain hypothesis supports the coherence-consciousness claim.

3. **Back-reaction mechanism** — analog gravity has no equivalent of "matter tells spacetime how to curve." Does the propagation framework have a derivation of back-reaction from coherence dynamics?
